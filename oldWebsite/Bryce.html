The Pieces

The Pawn

The pawn is the smallest piece of the game, however, you get 8 of
them. The pawn can start by either moving 1 space, or 2 spaces.
After it makes its first move, it. When the pawn wants to capture
an opponent peice, it has to move diagonally foward instead of
straight foward. If a pawn makes it to the end of the opponent's
side, it can become a bishop, knight, rook, or even a queen.

The Bishop

The bishop is one of the most simple pieces to move in the
entire game. They can only move diagnolly. They also capture
the enemy's pieces diagnolly, so they are
very easy to move. You get 2 of them at the beginning of every game,

The Knight

The knight is worth the same amount as the bishop and you
also get to of them at the beginning, however, the knight
is pretty complex to move. The knight moves in an L
shape. They are important, because they can perform forks, and other basic tactics.

The Rook

The rook is very similar to the bishop. The rooks start at the
very edges of the board, and move in a straight line
vertically or horizontally, but not diagnolly.
They are alson an important fragment in castling

The Queen

The queen is the most mobile piece in the game. It is basically
the rook, and the bishop combined. Because of the
queen's power you only get one at the start of the
game. The queen can move vertically, horizontally, and diagonally!

The King

The queen may be the most mobile piece in the game, but the
King is definitely the most important piece. If the king
is attacked, this is called a check, and you have to
save the king. The way the king moves, is the same as the
Queens, but he can only move 1 square in each direction.

Check(mate)

Check

A Check is simply an attack on the king. When the king is in check, or attacked, you must move him to safety. You can also use the acronym C.P.R, or capture, protect, run, to remember. However, if you are put in checked by a knight, you can only use C and R.

Checkmate

A checkmate is simply a check your king can't escape from. If you can't run, capture, or protect, you are in checkmate, and you lose. A lot of checkmates have names

1. Two Rook Mate
2. Queen Mate
3. Rook Mate
4. Scholar's Mate
5. Fool's Mate

Castling

Castling is a simple way to get a rook into the game, and move your king to safety. To do it, you have to make sure your king, and the intended rook to not have moved for the whole game, and the space between them is clear. Then, move the king two spaces toward the rook, and have the rook jump over the king

  1. Stalemate

    Stalemate is a draw when one side has no legal moves, but is not in check, and is actually a draw

Other unimportant rules

  1. En Passant

    En Passant is a strange rule, where if a pawn advances 2 squars from its starting position, right next to one of your pawns, you are alowed to take the pawn, but instead of going sideways, you go behind the pawn, and it still gets taken.

  2. Pawn Promotion

    Pawn Promotion is a rule, where when a pawn reaches the end of the board, it can become any other piece(except for the king)

The Ruy Lopez is probably the oldest Chess opening around. It starts with the basic King's pawn out 2 on both sides. White attacks black's weak pawn with his knight to f6 black defends with his knight. White atack's the defending knight with his bishop, in attempt to win the center pawn. 1.e4 e5 2.Nf3 Nc6 3.Bb5
